Business Tax (Self employed)

Self-employed income is that which you earn from your own business, whether it is a sole proprietorship or a partnership. Self-employment income must always be reported on your personal income tax return (T1) and never in your employment income. A person can report their business income based on a fiscal period, typically around December 31st (year- end).

If you are self-employed, the individual must keep accurate records of their income and expenses, as you are entitled to deduct the costs of earning your income. It is therefore pertinent that you maintain proof of your self-employment expenses to support your claims and avoid tax penalties.

Corporate Tax

A corporation must file their corporate income tax return (T2) after fiscal ending, which will be within 90 days from the end of the fiscal year regardless if there is no tax payable. As a company, it is important to keep in mind that the correctness of a corporate income tax return depends largely on the structure and accuracy of its bookkeeping records. Corporations can choose any fiscal ending date for the first year in business. Along with tax return, it also has to attach complete financial statements, GIFI, and the necessary schedules to the T2 return.
